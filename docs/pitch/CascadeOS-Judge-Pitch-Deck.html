01 Problem
Disconnected response

Infrastructure failures do not stay inside one agency.

A water incident can become a transit delay, a hospital access issue, and an emergency routing problem within minutes.

Today

Siloed screens

  • Water, MTA, hospitals, and responders see separate dashboards.
  • Teams react after impact becomes visible.
Risk

Hidden dependencies

  • The critical part is not one failure.
  • The critical part is what the failure touches next.
Need

Shared prediction

  • Officials need a single answer fast.
  • What failed, what is next, and what should move now.
Say this: The project focuses on the gap between agencies, because cascades happen in that gap.
02 Solution
One graph, four agents

CascadeOS turns live city signals into operational decisions.

How it works
  • Model NYC systems as a NetworkX directed graph.
  • Run domain agents for water, transit, health, and emergency response.
  • Predict cascade spread with weighted BFS.
  • Route responders around blocked nodes with Dijkstra.
What users get
  • Live NYC map with affected infrastructure.
  • Agent reasoning and priority alerts.
  • Cascade timeline and what-if simulation.
  • Claude generated city briefing for officials.
Say this: The system does not just show data. It converts data into a predicted impact chain and a recommended response.

06 Technical Depth
Algorithms and AI

The core is DSA plus agents, not just prompts.

Algorithms
  • NetworkX directed graph with 40 infrastructure nodes.
  • Betweenness centrality for node importance.
  • Weighted BFS for cascade spread.
  • Dijkstra for blocked-node rerouting.
  • Max heap for alert priority.
AI Layer
  • Claude Sonnet for agent reasoning and city briefings.
  • LangGraph orchestration across domain agents.
  • YOLOv8 for traffic camera anomaly signals.
  • Fallback detection keeps the demo reliable.
Say this: The AI is useful because it sits on top of real graph algorithms and live state.
